# Pocoyo  

**Name:** Pocoyo  

**Series:** Pocoyo (TV Series)  

**Creator(s):** Guillermo García Carsí, David Cantolla, Luis Gallego 

**First Appearance:** Pilot / early episode (commonly cited as *“Umbrella Umbrella”*)   

**Species:** Human (idealised preschool-aged boy)

**Age:** Approximately 4 years old (in early seasons) 

**Pronunciation / Name Meaning:** “Pocoyo” is a made-up term, derived from Spanish *poco yo* meaning “little me” — reflecting the character as a small self in discovery. 

---

## Appearance  
- Wears a blue three-flap hat, a blue jacket with a yellow zipper, and matching blue pants/shoes.  
- Black or very dark brown eyes, simple facial features.  
- Design is minimalist, the character stands out against mostly white backgrounds in the show, emphasising his actions and discovery. 

---

## Personality & Traits  
- Curious, playful, energetic, always ready to explore new things. 
- Innocent and cheerful, but also sometimes distracted, frustrated, jealous or disobedient — typical of a young child learning about the world.  
- Has strong friendships and cares about his friends, though he sometimes must learn social/emotional lessons. 
- Moves with acrobatic energy and speed (in the show’s logic) — more of a cartoon exploration style than realistic.

## Appearances  
### Key Roles & Formats  
- He is the **title and main character** of the “Pocoyo” series — nearly every episode centres on his interaction with the world and his friends. 
- His name appears in numerous spin-offs, specials and digital content — for example digital shorts, YouTube direct releases.

### Evolution  
- Early seasons portray Pocoyo more silent or minimally verbal; in later content he may talk more (depending on the version). 
- The show’s design and production evolved: from simple broadcast episodes to digital platforms, apps, and interactive content — meaning Pocoyo’s appearances extend beyond classic TV. 

---

## Trivia & Notes  
- Name origin: The creators based the term “pocoyo” on a phrase from a little girl’s prayer “eres niño poco yo” (you are a little-me) — according to creators’ notes. 
- Suggested birthday: According to some fan sources, Pocoyo’s birthday is October 14. 
- The visual style of the show (and Pocoyo’s design) emphasises minimalism so children focus on the action and relationships rather than richly detailed backgrounds. 

---

## Significance  
Pocoyo serves as a friendly “window” character for very young children — through his eyes, they explore the world, ask questions, make mistakes and learn. His simple design, cheerful nature and the show’s interactive narrator format make him especially effective for preschool learning and early childhood engagement.
