# Characters of Pocoyo

This page gathers major, recurring and supporting characters from the preschool show *Pocoyo*.  
Many characters appear across seasons, specials and spin-offs.

---

## Main / Core Characters

### Pocoyo  
- A young boy in blue, curious, playful, adventurous. 
- Often portrayed wearing a blue three-flap hat, jacket and pants. 
- Best friends include Pato, Elly, Loula, Sleepy Bird, Baby Bird. 
- Name derived from Spanish “poco yo” meaning roughly “little me”. 

### Elly  
- A pink elephant and one of Pocoyo’s close friends. 
- Often gentle, caring, sometimes cautious; often rides a scooter or plays politely.  

### Pato  
- A yellow duck friend of Pocoyo. 
- Frequently serves as a comedic foil or contrast to Pocoyo’s energy.  

### Loula  
- Pocoyo’s pet dog. 
- Though less vocal, appears regularly in adventures and as companion.  

### Nina  
- Introduced in later seasons, often depicted in green outfit and with red hair (in some versions). 
- Acts as a friend to Pocoyo and sometimes leads adventures of her own.  

---

## Recurring & Supporting Characters

### Sleepy Bird (also “Pajaroto” in Spanish)  
- A teal/green bird often seen half asleep, often disturbed by Pocoyo and friends.
- Introduced early in series; appears in many episodes.  

### Baby Bird („Pajarito” in Spanish)  
- Child of Sleepy Bird; more active and adventurous than the parent bird. 
### Caterpillar  
- A yellow caterpillar with a blue bow; speaks in gibberish and transforms into a butterfly.  

### Fred the Octopus (aka Pulpo)  
- A red octopus (often shown with 4 visible tentacles) who likes opera-singing and antics. 

### Angry Alien  
- An alien character from space, sometimes in antagonist role though comedic in tone. 

### Bea  
- Introduced in Season 5 as Pocoyo’s younger sister. Wears yellow with a blue flower; energetic and chaotic. 

### Narrator  
- The voice that addresses Pocoyo and the viewers directly; important for the show’s interactive tone.

## Others & Minor Characters  
- There are many minor, episodic or cameo characters (children, animals, alternate versions) which appear for single episodes or segments.  
- Examples: “The Children” group who answer the narrator’s questions. 
- Many animal friends, transformation characters, special guests and seasonal characters are present across the show’s long run.

---

## Notes on Completeness & Variation  
- Some character lists in different languages or regions differ: names may change (e.g., Pajaroto/Pájaro Dormilón for Sleepy Bird) and some characters may appear only in later seasons. 
- The show continues to evolve new characters (for example Bea in Season 5) so the list here might expand in future.
